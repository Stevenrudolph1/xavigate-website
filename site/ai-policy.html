AI Policy

What You Can Do

The material is designed to travel with whatever tools you use. That includes AI.

  • Load the books into AI tools. Claude, ChatGPT, whatever you work with. Analyze, summarize, ask questions, explore applications.
  • Have conversations about the frameworks. Test ideas, compare patterns, reflect on your own situations—however helps you engage with the work.
  • Apply it in your practice. If you hold a practitioner license, use AI to support client work, prepare sessions, develop your own materials.
  • Use it wherever you work. LMS, notes app, coaching platform, AI assistant—your call. The material travels with you.

What You Cannot Do

The following uses are prohibited without explicit written permission:

  • Train AI models on Xavigate content. You may not use the material to fine-tune, train, or otherwise improve AI systems.
  • Build commercial AI systems using the content. RAG systems, chatbots, or AI services that redistribute Xavigate content to others require licensing.
  • Create derivative commercial products via AI. Using AI to process and repackage the work into courses, programs, or tools for sale requires licensing.

Where the Line Is

If you're using AI to support your own learning and practice, that's personal use. Encouraged.

If you're building systems or services for others using the material, that's commercial use. That requires a license. The structure, language, and distinctions in this work took decades to develop. Personal use is free. Commercial redistribution isn't.

For Practitioners

If you hold a Practitioner License, AI is part of your toolkit—same as any other tool you use with clients. Prepare sessions, develop materials, support your work. What you cannot do is redistribute the material itself or create competing products.

Not sure if your use case requires additional licensing? Ask us. We'd rather help you use the work than create barriers to it.

Enforcement

We don't police personal use. If you own the material and you're working with it privately, there's nothing to enforce.

Commercial misuse is different. Building products, services, or systems for others using this material—through AI or otherwise—requires a license. Same rule whether you're using AI or doing it by hand.

Summary

Use AI however it helps. Load the books. Ask questions. Test ideas. The material travels with you.

Don't train models or redistribute the content. If you want to build something commercial, let's talk about licensing.
