Frameworks

Four frameworks. One system. Each addresses a different aspect of how people and structures relate.

Renergence™

Epistemic · Observational

Names what happens when sustained engagement stops returning more than it takes. Reveals how cost hides early and compounds over time.

Function: Notice when renergence fades—when what once returned more than it took now takes more than it returns.

Heroes Not Required

Load-Bearing · Prescriptive

Shows why constant heroics signal structural failure. When life requires sustained exceptionalism just to keep things running, the structure is unsafe.

Function: Recognize when heroics become normalized, and prescribe structural moves that relocate load from people into structure.

The Engagement Map

Practice · Positional

Maps the structural layer beneath practitioner skill. Shows how where you attend from governs what your skills can access.

Function: Understand practitioner positioning and response modes. Recognize which mode you're in and what each mode enables.

See books →

Multiple Natures®

MN Imprint · Fit Guidance

Understanding natural differences in how people engage—not as fixed types, but as expressed tendencies under contextual constraints that shape fit.

Function: Recognize that different people have different assets and liabilities in different situations. About fit with structure, not categorizing people.

See books →

How They Fit Together

All four address the same underlying reality: people operate inside systems, systems are structured, and structure shapes what becomes possible. Each framework provides a different lens on that relationship.

Renergence reveals whether something returns more than it takes, over time.

Heroes Not Required prescribes structural moves when heroics become normal.

The Engagement Map shows where practitioners stand and what becomes available from each position.

Multiple Natures clarifies how people differ in what energizes or drains them.
