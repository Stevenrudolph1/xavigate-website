Use Professionally

For coaches, consultants, educators, and organizations working with the Renergence framework.

Who This Is For

Practitioners who work with people in situations that cost more than they return—and who want a structural method rather than a conversational one.

  • Coaches who want to move past personality-based models toward structural diagnosis
  • Consultants who diagnose organizational dysfunction and need a framework for separating arrangement problems from people problems
  • Educators who work with engagement and fit—not just achievement
  • HR and organizational leaders who need to identify where structural load, misfit, or collapsed attention is generating cost

Training

Foundation Course

Covers the three diagnostic areas—how arrangements distribute load, how work fits or doesn’t fit the person doing it, and how attending position shapes what a practitioner can access. This is prerequisite to all applied courses.

Status: Enrolling now. See current training details.

Applied Courses

Separate courses cover each diagnostic area in depth. Each teaches the specific diagnostic moves, intervention design, and indicator specification for that area.

Status: Coming soon. Foundation course is prerequisite.

Usage Boundaries

What trained practitioners can do

  • Use the framework language with clients in professional contexts
  • Conduct structural diagnosis using the methods taught in training
  • Reference the books and framework in your own practice materials
  • Describe yourself as a Renergence-trained practitioner (after completing Foundation)

What requires separate licensing

  • Reproducing framework materials in your own publications or courses
  • Training others in the method
  • Using Renergence trademarks in your business name or branding
  • Building products or assessments on top of the framework

What no one should do

  • Present structural diagnosis as therapy
  • Use the framework to make clinical diagnoses
  • Claim the method replaces professional mental health treatment
  • Modify the diagnostic categories and present the result as Renergence
