Renergence

What you're in costs more than it returns.

Sometimes that's obvious—you can feel it every day and so can everyone around you. Sometimes it's hidden—things look fine, but the energy to maintain them isn't coming back. And sometimes it's just flat—not bad enough to leave, not good enough to stay. The job, the relationship, the commitment. It takes more than it gives.

You're not failing. The arrangement is expensive. And no one told you early enough to notice.

Structure

Does everything depend on someone being exceptional?

Maybe it's you—holding the family together, keeping the team running, carrying what should be built into the design. Maybe nobody's carrying it, and things just keep falling through. Maybe the roles exist on paper but nobody knows who actually decides. The structure isn't doing its job—so either people overcompensate, or nothing holds.

Competence gets mistaken for capacity. Gaps get mistaken for laziness. That's not a people problem. That's structural failure.

Alignment

The work doesn't fit. Whether you can tell or not.

Sometimes it's obvious—you hate the work and everyone can see it. Sometimes it's hidden—you're succeeding, but the energy to do it isn't coming from the work itself. And sometimes you're in between—tolerable, functional, but flat. The signal isn't whether you're good at it. It's whether it gives back what it takes.

Multiple Natures gives you a language for the range—so you can tell the difference between what works and what actually fits.
