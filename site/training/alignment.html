Alignment: When the Work Doesn't Fit

They're good at it. They're succeeding. And it's quietly costing them everything.

Some people are depleted not because the work is badly structured or because something is broken. The structure is fine. They're just good at work that fights who they are.

They show up, perform, deliver. Everyone says they're thriving. But the energy to do it isn't coming from the work — they're manufacturing it. And that manufacturing has a cost that nobody sees, sometimes not even the person paying it.

This course teaches you to see the cost. To tell the difference between "energized by the work" and "good at forcing themselves to show up." And to surface what's happening in a conversation without triggering a crisis — because the person often doesn't know this is happening to them.

How This Course Works

Curriculum

Module 1 — The Hidden Cost

Performance and fit are not the same thing. Someone can be excellent at work that slowly depletes them — and the excellence is exactly what hides the problem. This module teaches you to see the difference between energy that comes from the work and energy that's being manufactured to get through it.

  • What natural engagement looks like — and what manufactured engagement looks like
  • The signals that someone is paying a cost their performance doesn't show
  • Why "but I'm good at it" is the most dangerous sentence in alignment work
  • Mapping what energizes vs. what drains — even when the person can't tell
  • Exercises: Reading cost signals in real situations
Module 2 — The Conversation

Seeing the misfit isn't enough. You have to surface it in a way that the person can actually hear — without threatening their identity, their career narrative, or their sense of competence. This is the hardest conversation in practitioner work, and this module teaches you how to have it.

  • Why alignment conversations are threatening — and how to make them safe
  • Surfacing cost without saying "you're in the wrong job"
  • When to open the conversation and when to wait
  • What happens when the person already knows — and what happens when they don't
  • Case submissions: Full alignment analysis including the conversation plan
