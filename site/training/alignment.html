Alignment: When the Situation Doesn't Fit

Something about the situation doesn't fit who they are. Sometimes that's obvious. Sometimes it hides behind years of competence. Either way, it costs. This course uses the Multiple Natures model to show you exactly where the gap is.

Some people are depleted not because the work is badly structured or because something is broken. The structure is fine. They're just good at work that fights who they are.

They show up, perform, deliver. Everyone says they're thriving. But the energy to do it isn't coming from the work — they're manufacturing it. Every situation pulls for certain kinds of engagement. When what a person naturally supplies matches what the situation demands, energy comes back. When it doesn't, they spend energy just to show up — and that cost compounds over time, sometimes visibly, sometimes not.

This course teaches you to read that gap using the Multiple Natures model — a system for mapping the recurring pulls people move toward naturally (what engages them) vs. what they have to force. You'll learn to tell the difference between "energized by the work" and "good at pushing through it," and to surface what's happening in a conversation without triggering a crisis.

Curriculum

Module 1 — The Hidden Cost

Performance and fit are not the same thing. Someone can be excellent at work that slowly depletes them — and the excellence is exactly what hides the problem. This module teaches you the Multiple Natures model — nine engagement types that describe where people's energy naturally goes — and how to use it to read the difference between energy that comes from the work and energy that's being manufactured to get through it.

  • The nine engagement types — what situations pull for and what people naturally supply
  • Natural engagement vs. manufactured engagement — and how to tell the difference
  • The signals that someone is paying a cost their performance doesn't show
  • Why "but I'm good at it" is the most dangerous sentence in alignment work
  • Mapping what energizes vs. what drains — using MN to see what the person often can't
  • Exercises: Reading cost signals in real situations
Module 2 — The Conversation

Seeing the misfit isn't enough. You have to surface it in a way that the person can actually hear — without threatening their identity, their career narrative, or their sense of competence. This is the hardest conversation in practitioner work, and this module teaches you how to have it.

  • Why alignment conversations are threatening — and how to make them safe
  • Surfacing cost without saying "you're in the wrong job"
  • When to open the conversation and when to wait
  • What happens when the person already knows — and what happens when they don't
  • Case submissions: Full alignment analysis including the conversation plan
