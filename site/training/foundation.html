The Foundation: Diagnostic Seeing

Can this person see past the story people tell to what's actually going on?

Someone is depleted. Overloaded. Stuck. Everyone has a theory — they need better boundaries, more resilience, a new mindset.

Usually, everyone is wrong.

The real issue is almost never the person. It's a condition in the situation — something missing in how the work is set up, a mismatch between the work and who they are, or a pattern in how the practitioner is showing up. But the default move, every time, is to look at the person and explain what's wrong with them.

This course teaches you to stop doing that. By the end, you can tell whether the real issue is how the work is set up, how well it fits the person, or how someone is showing up in the room — and why getting that right changes everything that comes after.

What You Learn

  • The Renergence premise — why capable people deplete in situations that should work
  • Why everyone defaults to blaming the person — and how to stop doing it
  • The three areas where problems actually live: how the work is set up, how well it fits the person, and how you show up in the room
  • How to tell which area is actually driving a situation — with evidence, not guesswork
  • Catching yourself reverting to coach/therapist/manager defaults and replacing them with what's actually going on

Curriculum

Module 1 — The Misread

Why the default explanation is almost always wrong. You learn the Renergence premise — that depletion in capable people usually comes from a condition in the situation, not a deficit in the person — and start catching the moment where your own read goes sideways.

  • Why "the person is the problem" feels so convincing — and when it's wrong
  • The three areas where problems actually live
  • How to catch yourself mid-read when you're defaulting to the person
  • Exercises: Spotting the misread in real situations
Module 2 — Telling Them Apart

The hard part isn't knowing the three areas exist. It's telling which one is actually driving the situation when they all look similar on the surface. This module teaches the diagnostic distinctions that separate a structural problem from a fit problem from a positioning problem.

  • What each area looks like in practice — and how they mimic each other
  • The evidence that tells you which area is actually driving the situation
  • Why "it could be anything" is a cop-out — and how to commit to a read
  • Exercises: Differential analysis on cases that could go either way
Module 3 — Holding the Read

You've made a read. Now what? This module covers what happens after the diagnosis — how to hold your analysis under pressure, how to question it without abandoning it, and how to present what you're seeing in a way that lands.

  • How to present a structural read without triggering defensiveness
  • When to question your own analysis — and when to trust it
  • The difference between explaining what you see and proving you're right
  • Case submissions: Full diagnostic analysis on real situations
