Course 1: Basics — Diagnostic Seeing

Can this person see past the story people tell to what's actually going on?

The foundation. You learn why capable people deplete in situations that should work — and why everyone's first instinct (to look at the person) is usually wrong. By the end, you can tell whether the real issue is how the work is set up, how well it fits the person, or how someone is showing up in the room — and why getting that right changes everything.

What You Learn

  • The Renergence premise — why capable people deplete in situations that should work
  • Why everyone defaults to blaming the person — and how to stop doing it
  • The three areas where problems actually live: how the work is set up, how well it fits the person, and how you show up in the room
  • How to tell which area is actually driving a situation — with evidence, not guesswork
  • Catching yourself reverting to coach/therapist/manager defaults and replacing them with what's actually going on

What You Do

  • 3 modules, ~20-25 hours
  • 11 application exercises (spotting the real issue, telling similar-looking problems apart, checking your reads)
  • 5 fully worked case walkthroughs
  • 2 case submissions with expert review
  • No prerequisites

Supplementary reading: Renergence, Heroes Not Required (Ch 1-4), Multiple Natures (Ch 1-3), The Engagement Map (Ch 1-3). All free.

Prerequisite: None

Courses 2-4: Focus Tracks

Go deep in one area. Pick based on where your work lives.

All three Focus courses are equal — same length, same depth, same price. Each teaches everything you need to work in one area. You can take one, two, or all three.

Structure Focus

Find what's missing in how the work is set up — and design the fix.

  • The 5 things that go missing in organizations (unclear decisions, undefined roles, broken processes, missing authority, lost knowledge)
  • How to audit what people are carrying that the system should handle
  • Spotting when one person becomes the bottleneck — and why the system made it happen
  • Designing fixes that change the structure, not the person
  • 13 exercises, 5 worked cases, 2 submissions

Reading: Heroes Not Required, The Bottleneck Trap

Alignment Focus

See when the work drains someone — even when they're good at it and don't realize it's costing them.

  • Understanding what naturally energizes a person vs. what they have to force
  • Spotting when someone is spending energy to engage — not receiving energy from it
  • Why being great at something doesn't mean it fits — and how success hides the cost
  • How to surface the hidden cost in conversation without triggering a crisis
  • 13 exercises, 5 worked cases, 2 submissions

Reading: Multiple Natures

Positioning Focus

Notice when you're stuck in one way of showing up — and learn to move deliberately.

  • The different ways you can show up in a session or conversation — and when each one works
  • Catching when you've collapsed into one mode and can't get out of it
  • The three things that break trust in a practitioner relationship — and how to recover
  • Watching your own patterns in real time so you can adjust
  • 13 exercises, 5 worked cases, 2 submissions

Reading: The Engagement Map

MNTEST Practitioner Training

Learn to give, read, and use the MNTEST assessment in your work.

Alignment Focus teaches you to see when the work doesn't fit someone. This course teaches you to measure it — using MNTEST, the assessment tool that maps what naturally engages a person and what doesn't.

What You Learn

  • MNTEST administration — how to give the test
  • Profile interpretation — reading and explaining results
  • Orientation sessions — walking someone through their profile
  • Application contexts — education, coaching, organizational development, counseling

Details

  • $499 — self-paced, written review
  • Prerequisite: Alignment Focus (Completed)
  • Separate from the main training — this teaches you to use a specific tool
  • MNTEST credits purchased separately

Prerequisite: Alignment Focus

What Every Course Contains

  • Application exercises — practice spotting the real issue, telling similar-looking problems apart, and checking your reads against expert answers. 5-8 per course.
  • Default-catching practice — notice when you're reverting to what a coach, therapist, or manager would normally say — and replace it with what's actually going on.
  • Fully worked case walkthroughs — see the complete process from a raw situation through to finding the real issue, choosing the right response, and questioning your own read. 5-8 per course.
  • Structured submission prompts — designed to draw out real analytical work, not vague reflections.
  • Expert review — detailed feedback on your case submissions from Steven. Where your analysis landed, where it drifted, and what a sharper read would look like.

Already Know Multiple Natures?

MN lives inside the Renergence framework — it's the part about what naturally engages people. If you already know MN, your skills transfer directly — you're further along than you think.

Multiple Natures Revisited is a short, free book that explains how MN fits into the bigger picture. It connects what you already know to the full Renergence framework and shows where Structure and Positioning go beyond what MN covers.
