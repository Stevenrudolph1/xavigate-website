The Courses

Foundations in Renergence

Where is the problem actually coming from — and how do you tell?

When someone is depleted, stuck, or burning out, everyone looks at the person. This course teaches you to look at the situation instead. You learn the three areas where problems actually live — how the work is set up, how well it fits the person, and how you're showing up in the room — and how to tell which one is driving what you're seeing.

3 modules · 20-25 hours · No prerequisites

Focus Tracks — Pick Based on Where Your Work Lives

All three are equal — same length, same depth, same price. Each teaches everything you need to work in one area. Take one, two, or all three.

Structure: Finding What's Missing

When people carry too much because something is missing in how the work is set up. Learn to find the absence, name it, and design the fix so the situation holds without someone heroically filling the gap.

Full course details →

Alignment: When the Work Doesn't Fit

When the work doesn't fit who someone is — even when they're succeeding at it. The role demands one thing, but what naturally engages the person is something else. Learn to see the mismatch and name what's actually off.

Full course details →

Positioning: How You Show Up

How practitioners get stuck in one way of engaging — helping when they should step back, analyzing when they should act, withdrawing when they should stay in it. Learn to notice the pattern in real time and move deliberately.

Already Know Multiple Natures?

MN lives inside the Renergence framework — it's the part about what naturally engages people. If you already know MN, your skills transfer directly — you're further along than you think.

Multiple Natures Revisited is a short, free book that explains how MN fits into the bigger picture. It connects what you already know to the full Renergence framework and shows where Structure and Positioning go beyond what MN covers.

Everyone starts at the Foundation — the diagnostic seeing course is new even for experienced MN practitioners. But the Alignment course will feel like home.

If you want to use MNTEST professionally, the path is: Foundation → Alignment → MNTEST.

If you hold an existing MN Practitioner certification, your credential stands. Contact us about how it relates to RPT.
