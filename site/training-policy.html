Our Approach to Training

What you earn, how you earn it, and how we think about recognition.

What Completion Means

Completion reflects demonstrated competence in Renergence diagnosis — across Structure, Alignment, and Positioning — at the time of evaluation. You earn it by submitting case work that shows diagnostic seeing: the ability to identify conditions rather than defaulting to personal narratives about those same situations.

"Completed Renergence for Practitioners Training — 2026"

The year matters. It signals recency, not rank. Learning is temporal, not permanent.

Each submission receives detailed expert feedback. Cases that don't yet demonstrate diagnostic seeing are returned with specific guidance and up to two revisions.

What Completion Permits

You may

  • State that you completed the training: "Completed RPT (2026)"
  • Reference the training in your professional materials
  • Say you are trained in the Renergence framework
  • Describe that you use Renergence diagnosis in your work
  • Opt in to the public completers directory

You may not

  • Say you are "certified by" Renergence
  • Use "Official Renergence Practitioner" or similar titles
  • Imply endorsement, authorization, or affiliation
  • Use Renergence trademarks as credentials or professional titles

The distinction: you can describe what you did and what you use. You cannot claim a status that was never granted.

Why We Don't Certify

Diagnostic seeing is a skill, not a status. You develop it through practice and it sharpens or dulls depending on how you use it. Attaching a permanent credential to a temporal skill creates a false signal — it suggests that something completed once remains true indefinitely.

We don't certify people because certification implies we can guarantee what someone will do with what they learned. We can't. We can confirm that you completed rigorous training and demonstrated diagnostic seeing — across Structure, Alignment, and Positioning — at the time of completion. What you build with that skill is yours.

This isn't a limitation. It's a design choice. The Renergence framework works because it stays honest about what it can and cannot see. The same principle applies to how we recognize the people who learn it.

The Case Submission Process

Each course requires case submissions — structured analyses of real situations using the diagnostic methodology taught in that course.

What you submit

A case analysis following provided prompts. Each submission includes: the situation (anonymized), domain identification with evidence, analysis of the condition (Structure, Alignment, or Positioning), intervention logic, and a self-critique section where you examine what might be wrong with your own read.

What you receive back

Detailed diagnostic critique — not a score. Where your analysis demonstrated real seeing. Where it drifted into personal narrative. Where you missed a signal. Where your intervention addressed symptoms instead of the underlying condition. What the expert read would look like for your specific case.

How we evaluate

Every case submission is reviewed across six dimensions:

  • Domain identification — did you correctly identify which domain (Structure, Alignment, or Positioning) is operating?
  • Evidence quality — is the identification based on observable evidence or assumed from the narrative?
  • Condition analysis — did you describe the actual condition (structural absence, engagement mismatch, mode collapse) or drift into personality/motivation language?
  • Intervention logic — does the proposed intervention address the condition or the person?
  • Self-critique — did you identify what might be wrong with your own read?
  • Narrative discipline — did you maintain the diagnostic frame or collapse back into coaching/therapy/management defaults?

The standard

Pass — condition identified, evidence-based, self-critical. Completion recorded.

Revise — close, but drifts into narrative or misroutes the domain. Returned with specific feedback.

Does not pass — personal narrative presented as diagnosis.

The standard is diagnostic seeing, not perfection. We're looking for whether you can identify the condition underneath a situation — Structure, Alignment, or Positioning — rather than defaulting to the personal narrative. Not whether you agree with a particular read.

Resubmission Policy

  • First revision — included with the course. You receive specific feedback on what to address and resubmit
  • Second revision — available for a $100 resubmission fee. Same detailed feedback, same standard
  • If a case does not pass after the second revision: 90-day cooldown before retaking that course

The first revision is part of the learning process — most people need it. The fee on the second revision reflects the real cost of expert review and ensures submissions are taken seriously. The cooldown isn't punishment — it's time for the diagnostic seeing to develop through practice before trying again.

Completers Directory

We maintain a voluntary, factual record of individuals who completed the training. It is not a certification, endorsement, or authorization. It is a list of people who did the work.

How the directory works

  • Opt-in only — at completion, you choose whether to be listed publicly
  • Factual only — name, year, and courses completed. No ratings, no rankings, no badges
  • Free — no annual fee, no renewal, no subscription
  • Permanent — your completion record does not expire or require maintenance
  • Removable — you can request removal at any time

The directory exists because the training is more useful when the people who completed it can be found. That's its only purpose.
